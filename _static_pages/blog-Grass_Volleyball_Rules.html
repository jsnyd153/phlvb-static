Grass Volleyball Rules

Read up on the local rules for grass (old school rules)

Eastern Pennsylvania is home to the Pottstown Rumble, the largest grass volleyball tournament in the country. One of the things that makes Pottstown unique is playing by “Old School Rules.” These rules have been adopted for most grass tournaments in the eastern Pennsylvania area, and most Philadelphia Volleyball events are run by similar rules.

Equipment

  • Games are played on a 60x30 court
  • The Yellow Optix Volleyball is the official ball for Philadelphia Volleyball events. The new Pink GRX ball is slowly working it's way into circulation, but is still not our standard ball yet.
  • At no time may a player touch the net or poles.
  • If you touch the net after the ball is down and it's part of your follow-through, it's a net violation.
  • Hair and clothing is may touch the net. Hats and shoes are NOT permitted to touch the net.
  • Any equipment issues with the net or court (lines) results in an immediate re-do of the point. A player may not purposely cause such an issue (judgement made by the ref in a competitive environment).
  • If there are no antennas, the poles are considered the boundary line. If a ball passes outside of the poles, it is considered out of bounds.
  • A player may enter the opponents space, court, or free zone, provided that the player does not contact the net and it does not interfere with the opponent's play.

Serving/Serve receive

  • Let serves (ball contacting the net) are not permitted.

  • You cannot pass a serve with hands apart. If the ball is recieved with two hands, they must be touching.

  • No finger acton may be used while in serve receive. The ball must "pop" off the receiving surface, ball may not come to rest in any fashion. If using two hands; Hands must be touching or together when receiving the serve.

  • The service toss may be dropped anywhere with no fault once per point. A subsequent drop of the service toss results in a loss of serve.

  • Hair and clothing is may touch the net. Hats and shoes are NOT permitted to touch the net.

  • A team determined to be out of serving order will lose that serve

Scoring

  • Many pickup events will use rally scoring to 21, win by 2 points with a cap of 23. Teams switch sides on multiples of 7.
  • Many competitive events will use sideout scoring, meaning teams only score a point on their own serve.
  • Games are usually played to 11 (switching sides on multiples of 4),or 15 (switching sides on multiples of 5).

Attacking

  • No open hand tips/dinks are permitted. You may send the ball over using closed knuckles or other rigid hand action, but you may not use finger tips to redirect the ball over the net.

  • You may not hand set over the net. If the wind blows a hand set over the net, it is a violation.

Setting

  • If players hand set, both hands must contact the ball simultaneously.
  • While spin on the ball is not a sure method of determine a double contact (refs should look for simultaneous hand contact) if a set rotates more than 1 1/2 times in the air, it is usually indicative of a double contact.

Blocking

  • A block does not count as a touch.
  • You may not use fingertips to redirect the ball when blocking.

Blocking

  • A ball cannot be received with open hands, unless the ball is “hard driven”
  • An attack is only considered “hard driven” if the ball is hit on a continuous downward path. If the offensive driven ball arcs at all, or deflects off a block, and you use your open hands to pass it, it is a violation.
  • Just becasue a ball is hit above the net does not make it a hard driven ball.
