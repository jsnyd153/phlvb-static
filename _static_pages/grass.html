Grass is traditionally played as doubles which means you get opportunities to touch the ball on every play, and it also demands that you become a well rounded player to compete. You’re not only battling the other team, but also the elements — the wind, the sun, the imperfections in the ground. It challenges your agility and balance, and makes it especially exciting watching higher level players fly around the court.

Due to the lack of beaches in Pennsylvania, grass is very popular around here. Eastern Pennsylvania is home to the Pottstown Rumble, the largest grass volleyball tournament in the country. One of the things that makes Pottstown unique is playing by “Old School Rules.” These rules have been adopted for most grass tournaments in the region, and most Philadelphia Volleyball events are run by similar rules.

Key "Old School" rules

  • Large Court (30x60)
  • No Let serves
  • Block does not count as a touch
  • No open-hand tips, sets or pushed over the net
