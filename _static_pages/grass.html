Grass Volleyball

Picture this: the warm sun kissing your skin, a gentle breeze flowing through your hair, and the soft, lush grass beneath your feet.

What sets grass volleyball apart is its unique blend of sport, nature, and Chill Vibes™. Whether you're a seasoned player or just looking to have some fun, this version of volleyball has a charm that's hard to resist.

Grass is traditionally player as doubles (2v2) which means you get opportunities to touch the ball on every play, and it also demands that you become a well rounded player to compete. You’re not only battling the other team, but also the elements — the wind, the sun, the imperfections in the ground. It challenges your agility and balance, and makes it especially exciting watching higher level players fly around the court.

What truly makes grass volleyball special, is that despite it being an athletically demanding sport, it tends to have a more relaxed vibe. You can usually find players hanging out in the sun with a beer in hand (at locations that allow it) while waiting their turn to play. It’s great as a way to wind down after work, or to hang out with friends on the weekend.

Old School Rules

Eastern Pennsylvania is home to the Pottstown Rumble, one of the largest annual grass volleyball tournament in the country. One of the things that makes Pottstown unique is playing by “Old School Rules.” These rules have been adopted for most grass tournaments in the region, so Philadelphia Volleyball runs most of our events by similar rules. Some of the key features of Old School Rules are:

  • Large Court (30x60)

  • No let serves

  • Block is not a touch

  • No open hand receives (unless hard driven)

  • No open-hand tips, sets over the net or No open-hand tips, sets over the net or directional blocking

  • Some events may use side out scoring.
