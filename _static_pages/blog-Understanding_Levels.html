Understanding Levels

Why levels matter

Making sure you are the proper level for an event is important for ensuring everyone stays safe and has a good time, but they can be confusing if you’re new to volleyball or new to the region.

Making sure you are the proper level for an event is important for ensuring everyone stays safe and has a good time, but they can be confusing if you’re new to volleyball or new to the region.

From a fun perspective, it is important for players to have the level of play they expect. loosing over and over isn’t fun, having players on your team that are weighing everyone else down or unable to play their position isn’t fun. Some events may have a wider range or all skill levels and tend to be more of a learning environment.

While all versions of volleyball use the same core skills, you can be different levels in different formats since they prioritize different things — i.e. grass doubles prioritizes being a well rounded player/smart and scrappy, and indoor 6s prioritizes being more specialized/power.
